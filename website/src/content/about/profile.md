Hey, I'm **Amr** — a full stack developer and web security researcher from **Gharbiya, Egypt**.

I'm currently a student at **Gharbiya STEM High School**, a public STEM school in Egypt that focuses on cultivating the next generation of scientists and engineers. Alongside my studies, I build real websites for real institutions, hunt vulnerabilities on PortSwigger, and lead a community of future hackers and builders as the **Leader of Gharbiya STEM Hack Club**.

My approach is hands-on and curiosity-driven. I believe the best way to learn a system is to understand how it works — and then figure out how it breaks. That philosophy carries through everything I do, whether I'm architecting a Django backend, designing a front-end with Astro, or grinding through PortSwigger labs to sharpen my security skills.

When I'm not writing code or hunting bugs, you'll find me running creative workshops for my Hack Club members — sessions like *Flavorless* and *Boba* that teach technical concepts in fun, hands-on ways — or organizing events at my school to inspire the next generation of builders.

I'm also building tools for the security community, including **Spidey** (a multithreaded web crawler), **SocketMapper** (for mapping open sockets), and writing about security on Medium.

> Understand the system. Then understand how it breaks.
